# Pain Points Analysis

## Objective

Identify operational challenges faced by the Officer of the Shift (OOTS) and evaluate opportunities for AI-assisted automation and decision support.

---

## Pain Point 1: Manual Task Assignment

### Current Process

The OOTS manually reviews incoming emails, tickets, and requests before assigning them to team members.

Assignment decisions are based on:

* Previous ownership
* Engineer skill level (L1/L2/L3)
* Current workload
* Shift availability

### Challenges

* Time-consuming decision making
* Reliance on tribal knowledge and experience
* Uneven workload distribution
* Risk of assigning work to unavailable engineers

### Impact

* Delayed task ownership
* Reduced operational efficiency
* Increased workload on OOTS

---

## Pain Point 2: Lack of Workload Visibility

### Current Process

The OOTS often does not have a real-time view of engineer workload.

Engineers may:

* Already have multiple open tasks
* Be attending meetings
* Be working on complex incidents

### Challenges

* Difficult to balance workload fairly
* Frequent reassignment requests
* Increased task delays

### Impact

* Slower ticket resolution
* Reduced team productivity

---

## Pain Point 3: Delayed Task Completion Across Shifts

### Current Process

Tasks are sometimes handed over between multiple shifts before completion.

### Challenges

* Lack of ownership continuity
* Delayed resolution
* Repeated context switching

### Impact

* Longer resolution times
* Increased operational overhead

---

## Pain Point 4: P1 Incident Detection and Correlation

### Current Process

When a site outage occurs, multiple incident tickets may be generated simultaneously.

The OOTS must manually:

* Identify patterns
* Verify outages
* Escalate incidents
* Create parent incidents

### Challenges

* High ticket volume
* Time pressure during outages
* Manual incident correlation

### Impact

* Slower major incident response
* Increased risk of human error

---

## Pain Point 5: Stakeholder Communication

### Current Process

The OOTS manually prepares stakeholder communications during major incidents.

### Challenges

* Repetitive communication tasks
* Time pressure during outages
* Need for accurate and consistent messaging

### Impact

* Administrative overhead
* Reduced focus on incident management

---

## Pain Point 6: Shift Handover Preparation

### Current Process

The OOTS manually gathers information from multiple systems before creating shift handovers.

### Challenges

* Information scattered across emails, tickets, and chat channels
* Risk of missing important updates
* Time-consuming process

### Impact

* Incomplete handovers
* Knowledge transfer gaps
* Increased workload during shift transitions

---

## Initial AI Opportunities

1. Smart Task Assignment Assistant
2. Workload Balancing Engine
3. Task Delay Prediction
4. Incident Flood Detection
5. Stakeholder Communication Generator
6. AI Handover Generator
